MOVIES/FILMS

Straight Outta Compton

- Although the movie is more focused about the come up of the late 80’s rap group the NWA, the movie does show police brutality and obvious cases of white privilege

- Available for rent

13th

- Main focus is on mass incarceration

- Comprehensive review of history of systemic racism starting in the 1920s to today

- Available on Netflix

When They See Us

- Central Park Jogger Case

- Focus on injustices of criminal justice system that disproportionately affect black americans (i.e., plea deals)

- Available on Netflix

Selma

- Historical film about Civil rights movement and how it lead to signing of Voting Rights Act

- Available for rent

I Am Not Your Negro

- "Remember This House" is a book written by James Baldwin about the personal account of the lives and assassinations of Medgar Evers, Malcolm X and Martin Luther King, Jr; upon Baldwin’s death, it was still incomplete. This film is a continuation of the book James Baldwin never finished

- Connects Civil Rights movement to BLM

LA 92

- LA riots that occurred to due Rodney King verdict

- Rodney King was a black man who was brutally beat by police officers during his arrest in 1991

- Available on Netflix

Teach Us All

- Racial segregation and discrmination in school systems

- Available on Netflix

Black American Since MLK: And Still I Rise

- Two-part series (total of 4 hours)

- Henry Louis Gates Jr presents last 50 years of black history through personal lens

Time: The Kalief Browder Story

- Story of Kalief Browder, an African American man who was charged for stealing a backpack. He refused the plea deals knowing he was innocent, but couldn’t pay bail, and was put in jail for three years for a charge that was never prosecuted in the end

- Available on Netflix
